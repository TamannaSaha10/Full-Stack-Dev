Mount Fuji

PMount Fuji, or Fuji-san, is Japan’s tallest mountain, standing at 3,776 meters (12,388 feet). Located on Honshu Island, it is an active stratovolcano that last erupted in 1707. Known for its symmetrical cone shape, Mount Fuji is a cultural icon of Japan, often depicted in art and photography. It is also a popular destination for climbers and tourists, especially during the summer months when thousands ascend to its peak

Blue Bird

Bluebirds are songbirds named for the males’ bright blue feathers. The soft whistles of their song are among the earliest sounds of spring in North America. Bluebirds belong to the thrush family. This means that they are related to thrushes, robins, and nightingales. There are three species, or types, of bluebird: the eastern bluebird, the western bluebird, and the mountain bluebird.

Gerbera

Gerbera is a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, Asteraceae. Known for their large, vibrant blooms, Gerberas come in a variety of colors including pink, yellow, orange, red, and white. They are popular in floral arrangements and gardens due to their striking appearance and long-lasting flowers
